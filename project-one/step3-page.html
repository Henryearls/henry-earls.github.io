3. Go (Weitz's Four Broad Areas of Inquiry)

Morris Weitz's framework of four broad areas of inquiry—description, interpretation, theorization, and critical assessment—provides a valuable lens through which to analyze Plato's Allegory of the Cave, Divided Line, and Sun. These allegories serve as foundational pillars in Plato's exploration of truth and beauty, inviting readers to contemplate the nature of reality and the human condition.
